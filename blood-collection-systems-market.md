Serum separating tubes are designed to separate serum from blood cells, while EDTA tubes contain an anticoagulant to prevent clotting. Plasma separation tubes are used to separate plasma from blood cells. Other types of tubes include heparin tubes for preventing clotting and glucose tubes for measuring glucose levels. Each type of tube serves a specific purpose in collecting and analyzing blood samples for diagnostic or research purposes.</p></p>

<p><p>The blood collection systems market is focused on two main applications - venous blood collection and capillary blood collection. Venous blood collection involves taking blood samples by puncturing a vein, typically in the arm, in order to diagnose various diseases or conditions. Capillary blood collection, on the other hand, involves pricking the skin, usually in the fingertip, to obtain a small amount of blood. Both methods are important in medical diagnostics and help healthcare professionals monitor patients' health and detect any abnormalities or infections.</p></p>
