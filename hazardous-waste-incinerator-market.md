<p><p>Hazardous Waste Incinerators are categorized based on the types of waste they process: liquid, solid, and gas. Liquid waste incinerators handle hazardous liquids, such as chemicals or solvents, and burn them at high temperatures to neutralize their harmful properties. Solid waste incinerators deal with hazardous solid materials, like contaminated soils or industrial waste, by subjecting them to intense heat until they are reduced to ash. Gas waste incinerators focus on gaseous hazardous waste, such as industrial emissions or toxic fumes, which are burned to eliminate harmful pollutants before they are released into the atmosphere.</p></p>
