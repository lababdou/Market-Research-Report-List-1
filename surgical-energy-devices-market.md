<p><p>The surgical energy devices market encompasses various types of devices, including monopolar, bipolar, ultrasonic, and others. Monopolar devices use a single electrode to deliver electrical current for cutting or coagulation purposes. Bipolar devices, on the other hand, use two electrodes for localized tissue coagulation. Ultrasonic devices employ high-frequency vibrations to cut and coagulate tissue simultaneously, reducing blood loss and improving precision. Other types of surgical energy devices may include radiofrequency devices or laser devices, offering alternative surgical techniques to achieve desired outcomes.</p></p>
