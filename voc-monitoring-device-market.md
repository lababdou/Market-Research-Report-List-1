<p><p>VOC monitoring devices are used to measure the levels of volatile organic compounds in various environments. Two commonly used types of devices in this market are PID (Photoionization Detector) and metal-oxide semiconductor sensors. PID devices use ultraviolet light to detect VOCs and provide real-time measurements. Metal-oxide semiconductor sensors use a heated metal-oxide film to detect VOCs by changes in electrical conductivity. Both types of devices are popular in industries where VOC exposure is a concern, such as manufacturing, chemical processing, and indoor air quality monitoring.</p></p>
