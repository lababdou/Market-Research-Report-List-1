<p><p>Micro fuel cells are a type of fuel cell technology that generate electricity through the chemical reaction between hydrogen and oxygen. There are two main types of micro fuel cells: air-cooled and liquid-cooled. In air-cooled micro fuel cells, a fan or a forced air system is used to cool down the fuel cell by blowing air over its surface. On the other hand, liquid-cooled micro fuel cells use a liquid cooling system, typically circulating coolant around the fuel cell to regulate temperature. Both types have their advantages and are used in various applications.</p></p>
