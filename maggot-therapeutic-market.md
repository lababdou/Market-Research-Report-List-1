<p><p>Maggot therapy, also known as maggot debridement therapy (MDT), is a form of biotherapy that involves the use of sterilized larvae of certain species of flies to clean wounds and promote healing. The larvae are placed on the wound, where they eat away dead tissue and bacteria, secreting antimicrobial substances and promoting healthy tissue growth. This therapy has been increasingly utilized as an alternative treatment for chronic and hard-to-heal wounds such as diabetic foot ulcers, venous leg ulcers, and pressure sores.</p><p>The market for maggot therapy has witnessed steady growth over the years, driven by factors such as the growing prevalence of chronic wounds, increasing awareness about the benefits of MDT, and the rise in antibiotic-resistant infections.
