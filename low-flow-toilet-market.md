<p><p>Low-flow toilets are categorized into two main types, siphonic and wash-down models. Siphonic toilets use a siphoning action to flush waste down the drain, while wash-down toilets rely on water pressure to push waste out through the trapway. Both types are designed to use less water per flush than traditional toilets, making them more environmentally friendly and cost-effective. The market for low-flow toilets is rapidly growing as consumers seek to reduce water usage and save on their water bills.</p></p>
