<p><p>The Wireless Electronic Health Records (EHR) market is comprised of various types of technologies that enable the wireless transmission and exchange of medical records. These include: </p><p>1) 3G/Commercial Cellular: Utilizes cellular networks to transmit EHR data securely and efficiently.</p><p>2) Bluetooth: Enables wireless communication between devices in close proximity, allowing for the transfer of EHR information.</p><p>3) Radio Frequency Identification (RFID): Uses electromagnetic fields to automatically identify and track medical objects or individuals, aiding in inventory management and patient tracking.</p><p>4) Satellites/Sensors: Utilizes satellite technology or sensors to transmit EHR data over long distances, especially useful in remote areas.</p><p>5) WiMax: Provides high-speed wireless communications over a wide area, facilitating quick transfer of large EHR files.</p><p>6) Wireless LANs: Local area networks that enable wireless EHR transfer within a specific area, such as a hospital or clinic.</p><p>7) Zigbee: A low-power wireless technology that allows for communication between devices in a secure and efficient manner, ideal for monitoring patients in healthcare settings.</p></p>
