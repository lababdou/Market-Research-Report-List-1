<p><p>Ophthalmic medical devices are categorized into three classes based on their level of risk and regulation. Class I devices, the lowest risk category, include products like eye drops and contact lenses. Class II devices, higher risk than Class I, include instruments like diagnostic equipment and intraocular lenses. Class III devices, the highest risk category, include surgical instruments and implants. These devices require the most stringent regulations and undergo rigorous testing and clinical trials to ensure their safety and effectiveness in treating eye conditions.</p></p>
