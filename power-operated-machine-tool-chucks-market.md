<p><p>Power operated machine tool chucks are a type of mechanism used to hold and clamp various tools or workpieces in a machine tool. The market for these chucks is segmented into different types based on the number of jaws: 2-jaw, 3-jaw, and 4-jaw. </p><p>- 2-jaw chucks have only two jaws that move in unison, providing a self-centering grip on the workpiece.</p><p>- 3-jaw chucks are the most common and versatile, with three jaws that move simultaneously to center the workpiece accurately.</p><p>- 4-jaw chucks have four independently moving jaws, allowing for greater flexibility in clamping irregularly shaped or non-symmetrical workpieces.</p></p>
