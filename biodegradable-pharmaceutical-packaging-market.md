<p><ul><li>Small Molecule</li><li>Large Molecule (Biologics)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The biodegradable pharmaceutical packaging market is categorized into two types: small molecule and large molecule (biologics). Small molecule drugs are typically synthetic compounds and are made using chemical processes. They are usually solid in nature and can be easily packaged in traditional packaging materials. On the other hand, large molecule drugs, also known as biologics, are produced using biotechnology processes and are usually proteins or antibodies. These drugs are more sensitive and require specialized packaging materials to maintain their stability and effectiveness.</p></p>
