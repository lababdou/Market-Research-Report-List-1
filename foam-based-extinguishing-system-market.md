<p><p>A foam-based extinguishing system is a type of fire protection system that utilizes foam as a firefighting agent. It is commonly used in areas where flammable liquid fires are a concern, such as chemical plants, refineries, and storage facilities.</p><p>Foam-based extinguishing systems work by applying a layer of foam on the surface of a flammable liquid, blocking the oxygen supply and suppressing the fire. They can be classified into two types - low expansion foam systems and high expansion foam systems. Low expansion foam systems produce a foam with low expansion ratio and are suitable for enclosed spaces, while high expansion foam systems produce a foam with high expansion ratio and are suitable for open areas.</p><p>The foam-based extinguishing system market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. <p><p>Foam-based extinguishing systems are categorized into three types based on the expansion ratio of the foam generated during firefighting. The low expansion foam-based extinguishing system produces foam with an expansion ratio of 20:1, suitable for confined spaces. The medium expansion foam-based extinguishing system generates foam with an expansion ratio of 20-200:1, ideal for open areas. The high expansion foam-based extinguishing system produces foam with an expansion ratio of over 200:1, suitable for large-scale fire incidents. These systems effectively suppress fires by covering and smothering them with a thick layer of foam.</p></p>
