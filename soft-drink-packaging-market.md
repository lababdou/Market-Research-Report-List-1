<p><p>Soft drink packaging can be categorized into two main types: rigid packaging and flexible packaging. </p><p>Rigid packaging refers to containers made from sturdy materials such as glass, plastic, or metal. These containers offer durability and protection to soft drinks during transportation and storage. Rigid packaging is commonly seen in the form of bottles or cans and is typically associated with traditional soft drink brands.</p><p>On the other hand, flexible packaging involves materials like plastic films, pouches, or bags. This type of packaging offers lightweight and convenient options for soft drink products. Flexible packaging is often used by newer or smaller brands, as it allows for customizable shapes and sizes, cost-effective manufacturing, and easier portability.</p><p>In summary, rigid packaging entails solid and durable containers like bottles or cans, while flexible packaging involves lightweight materials such as plastic films or pouches, offering convenience and flexibility for soft drink products.</p></p>
