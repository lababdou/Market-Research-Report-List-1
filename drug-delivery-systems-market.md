<p><p>Drug Delivery Systems (DDS) refer to various techniques and technologies used to administer medications to patients effectively. Intrauterine implants involve the insertion of small devices containing medication into the uterus. Prodrug implants involve using inactive compounds that are metabolized into active drugs once implanted. Polymeric DDS utilize biocompatible polymers to release drugs gradually over time. Targeted DDS specifically deliver drugs to targeted cells or tissues in the body. Other types of DDS include inhalation, transdermal, and oral delivery systems. These systems enhance therapeutic outcomes, reduce side effects, and improve patient compliance.</p></p>
