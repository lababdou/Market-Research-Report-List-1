<p><p>Notebook computer batteries consist of two main types: rechargeable batteries and non-rechargeable batteries. Rechargeable batteries are designed to be recharged multiple times and provide a longer lifespan. They are commonly used in notebooks, as they can be charged easily using a power outlet or USB connection. On the other hand, non-rechargeable batteries are used only once and cannot be recharged. They provide a limited lifespan and are often used as backup or emergency options in case of power outages or when access to a power source is not possible.</p></p>
