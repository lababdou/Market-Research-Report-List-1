<p><p>High Pressure Processing (HPP) is a non-thermal food processing technique that involves the use of high hydrostatic pressure to eliminate harmful bacteria, extend product shelf life, and preserve the sensory and nutritional qualities of food. The process subjects the product to pressures typically ranging from 400 to 600 MPa for a short period, ensuring food safety without compromising its quality.</p><p>The High Pressure Processing (HPP) market is witnessing steady growth due to the increasing demand for minimally processed and additive-free foods, coupled with the rising consumer awareness about food safety and quality. Additionally, the advantages offered by HPP, such as increased shelf life, improved freshness, and reduced reliance on synthetic preservatives, are further driving market growth.</p><p>The market is also witnessing the emergence of new applications for HPP in the production of juices, beverages, and meat products. HPP is particularly gaining popularity in the fresh juice segment, as it helps maintain the organoleptic characteristics of the final product without compromising on safety. Moreover, the technology is also being adopted by manufacturers to produce clean-label and natural products that meet the growing consumer demand for healthier and more sustainable food options.</p><p>According to market research, the global High Pressure Processing (HPP) market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.4% during the forecast period.
