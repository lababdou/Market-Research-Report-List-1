<p><p>Medical contrast agents are substances used to enhance the visibility of specific tissues or organs in medical imaging procedures such as X-rays, CT scans, MRI, and ultrasound. There are four main types of contrast agents available in the market: Barium-based contrast media, which is commonly used in X-rays of the gastrointestinal tract; Iodinated contrast media, used in CT scans and angiography procedures; Gadolinium-based contrast media, utilized in MRI scans; and Microbubble contrast media, used in ultrasound imaging for assessing blood flow and tissue perfusion. Each type serves a specific purpose in improving the accuracy and quality of medical imaging studies.</p></p>
