<p><ul><li>Root Form Dental Implant</li><li>Plate Form Dental Implant</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Narrow dental implants refer to the size of the implant used in dental procedures. There are two types of narrow dental implants: root form and plate form. Root form dental implants are cylindrical in shape and resemble the root of a natural tooth. They are used when the jawbone is deep and wide enough to support them. Plate form dental implants, on the other hand, are flat and long, suitable for narrow jawbones or when there is limited bone width. Both types of implants are commonly used to replace missing teeth and provide support for dental prosthetics.</p></p>
