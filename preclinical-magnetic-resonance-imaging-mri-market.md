<p><p>Preclinical Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is a medical imaging technique used in research on animals before clinical trials. It is typically categorized into two market types: Structural Imaging and Functional Imaging. Structural Imaging enables researchers to visualize and analyze the anatomy and structure of the animal subject. Functional Imaging, on the other hand, captures the dynamic changes in the animal's physiology and allows researchers to study its functional processes such as blood flow, metabolism, and brain activity. These types of imaging techniques aid in the understanding of disease mechanisms and the development of new therapeutic approaches.</p></p>
