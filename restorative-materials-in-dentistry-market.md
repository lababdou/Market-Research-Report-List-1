<p><p>Restorative materials play a crucial role in dentistry, offering solutions to replace or restore damaged or decayed teeth. Ceramic restorative materials are popular due to their natural appearance and excellent strength. Amalgam, made from a mixture of metals, is durable and cost-effective but lacks aesthetic appeal. Composite materials provide good aesthetics and can be bonded directly to the tooth, though they may not be as durable as ceramics or amalgam. Other restorative materials include metals and glass ionomers, each with their own advantages and applications in dental restoration.</p></p>
