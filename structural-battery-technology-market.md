<p><p>Structural Battery Technology refers to the integration of batteries into the structure of a device or system, serving the dual purpose of energy storage and load-bearing functionality. There are two main types of Structural Battery Technology: Nickel-based Technology and Lithium-based Technology. Nickel-based Technology involves the use of nickel-cadmium or nickel-metal hydride batteries, known for their high energy density and long cycle life. On the other hand, Lithium-based Technology utilizes lithium-ion or lithium-polymer batteries, which offer higher energy density and longer shelf life compared to nickel-based alternatives.</p></p>
