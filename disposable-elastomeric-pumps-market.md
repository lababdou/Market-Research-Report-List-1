<p><p>Disposable elastomeric pumps are medical devices used for continuous or intermittent infusion of medications or fluids into a patient's body. These pumps consist of a collapsible reservoir made of elastomeric material, allowing the gradual release of the medication or fluid at a controlled rate. They are widely used in ambulatory and home care settings for the administration of pain medication, antibiotics, chemotherapy drugs, and other substances.</p><p>The disposable elastomeric pumps market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>The Disposable Elastomeric Pumps market consists of two main types: Continuous Rate Elastomeric Pumps and Variable Rate Elastomeric Pumps. Continuous Rate Elastomeric Pumps are devices that deliver a constant flow rate of medication or fluids to the patient. They are commonly used for long-term treatments, such as chemotherapy or pain management. On the other hand, Variable Rate Elastomeric Pumps allow for adjustable flow rates, providing flexibility in dosage delivery. They are often used for shorter-term treatments and in situations where dosage requirements may change over time.</p></p>
