<p><ul><li>Gas Turbine</li><li>Steam Turbine</li><li>Reciprocating Engine</li><li>Fuel Cell</li><li>Microturbine</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The commercial cogeneration system market consists of various types: gas turbine, steam turbine, reciprocating engine, fuel cell, and microturbine. Gas turbine cogeneration systems use gas turbines to generate electricity and recover waste heat for heating or cooling purposes. Steam turbine systems utilize steam turbines to produce electricity by utilizing steam generated from a heat source. Reciprocating engine cogeneration systems involve engines that generate electricity and recover the waste heat. Fuel cell systems use chemical reactions to generate electricity and produce heat as a byproduct. Finally, microturbine cogeneration systems utilize small gas turbines for electricity and heat production.</p></p>
