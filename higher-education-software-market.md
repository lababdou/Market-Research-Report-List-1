<p><p>The higher education software market can be classified into two types: cloud-based and on-premises. Cloud-based software refers to applications that are hosted and accessed over the internet, eliminating the need for local installation or maintenance. On-premises software, on the other hand, is installed and operated on the organization's own servers or infrastructure. Cloud-based software offers flexibility, scalability, and cost-effectiveness, while on-premises software provides more control and data security. The choice between these two types depends on the specific needs and preferences of the higher education institution.</p></p>
