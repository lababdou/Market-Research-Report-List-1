<p><p>Poultry vaccines are categorized into two types: liquid vaccines and freeze-dried vaccines. Liquid vaccines are formulated in a liquid form and are easy to administer, usually through drinking water or injection. On the other hand, freeze-dried vaccines are made in a solid form and require rehydration before administration. They have a longer shelf life and are more stable during transportation and storage. Both types of vaccines play a crucial role in protecting poultry birds from various diseases and ensuring their health and productivity.</p></p>
