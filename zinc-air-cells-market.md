<p><p>Zinc Air Cells are batteries that use zinc and oxygen as reactants to generate electricity. They are available in three market types: primary (non-rechargeable), secondary (rechargeable), and mechanical recharge. Primary cells are disposable and cannot be recharged, making them ideal for devices with low power needs. Secondary cells, on the other hand, can be recharged multiple times, making them more suitable for high-power devices. Mechanical recharge market involves replacing spent zinc anodes in the cell, allowing it to be reused, providing a cost-effective and sustainable solution for certain applications.</p></p>
