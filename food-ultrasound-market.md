<p><p>The Food Ultrasound Market includes two types: high-frequency low-intensity and low-frequency high-intensity. High-frequency low-intensity ultrasound is characterized by using high-frequency sound waves with low power levels. This type is commonly employed for non-destructive testing and food quality control purposes. On the other hand, low-frequency high-intensity ultrasound utilizes low-frequency sound waves but at higher power levels. This type is mainly implemented for applications such as food processing, emulsification, and extraction, where more intense and powerful ultrasound is required for efficient results.</p></p>
