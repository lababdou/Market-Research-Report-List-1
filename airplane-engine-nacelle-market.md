<p><p>The airplane engine nacelle market comprises two main types: wing-mounted and rear-mounted. In the wing-mounted configuration, the nacelles are positioned beneath the wings of the aircraft. This placement offers advantages such as improved aerodynamics, reduced drag, and enhanced fuel efficiency. On the other hand, rear-mounted nacelles are positioned at the rear of the fuselage of the aircraft. This configuration requires different engineering considerations, such as maintaining a balanced weight distribution and ensuring proper airflow.
