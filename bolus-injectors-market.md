<p><p>Bolus injectors are devices used to deliver a single dose of medication or fluid to patients. They are available in various types including electronic, mechanical, hydrogel, and hydraulic market. </p><p>Electronic bolus injectors are powered by batteries and offer programmable dosage control. Mechanical injectors use a spring and manual triggering mechanism for dose delivery. Hydrogel injectors are designed with a swelling polymer that gradually releases medication over time. Hydraulic injectors use fluid pressure to control dose delivery. Each type of bolus injector has its specific features and is utilized based on the patient's requirements and medical scenarios.</p></p>
