<p><p>Multiparameter patient monitors are electronic devices used in healthcare settings to continuously monitor vital signs and other parameters of patients. High-acuity monitors are designed for critical care units and provide extensive monitoring capabilities, including ECG, blood pressure, temperature, and oxygen saturation. Mid-acuity monitors are suitable for general care areas and offer fewer parameters and functionalities compared to high-acuity monitors. Low-acuity monitors are basic devices used in non-urgent care settings, focusing mainly on vital signs such as blood pressure and pulse rate. Each type of monitor caters to different levels of patient care needs.</p></p>
