<p><p>Medical insurance market types refer to different ways in which healthcare costs and liabilities are shared among various entities. Insured liability is a market where individuals or employers purchase insurance plans to cover their medical expenses, and the insurance company assumes the liability for those costs. On the other hand, the payment method market involves various ways in which healthcare providers are reimbursed for their services. This can include fee-for-service, where providers are paid for each service rendered, or capitation, where providers receive a fixed amount per patient regardless of the services provided.</p></p>
