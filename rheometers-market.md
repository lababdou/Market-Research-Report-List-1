<p><p>Rheometers are devices used to measure the flow properties and viscoelastic behavior of materials. The market for rheometers can be classified into different types. Dynamic rheometers measure the viscoelastic behavior of materials under varying conditions. Capillary rheometers evaluate the flow properties of polymer melts through a capillary die. Torque rheometers measure the torque required to rotate a spindle in a viscous material. Other types of rheometers include oscillatory rheometers and rotational rheometers. Each type serves specific purposes in analyzing and characterizing the rheological properties of materials.</p></p>
